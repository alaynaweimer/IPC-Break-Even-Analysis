üéØ Why Break-Even Matters

Your break-even point is your survival number. It's the minimum you need to sell just to keep the lights on ‚Äî before you make a single dollar of profit.

Knowing this number lets you set realistic sales goals, make smarter pricing decisions, and understand when a slow month is just a dip versus a crisis.

üéØ The goal: Get past break-even as fast as possible each month. Everything after that is profit in your pocket.

üìê The Break-Even Formula

Break-even analysis answers one question: How many units (clients, projects, products) do I need to sell to cover all my costs?

Break-Even = Fixed Costs √∑ (Price ‚àí Variable Cost)
The result is the number of sales needed to cover your costs

The "(Price ‚àí Variable Cost)" part is called your contribution margin ‚Äî it's how much each sale contributes toward covering your fixed costs.

üè∑Ô∏è Fixed vs. Variable Costs

Understanding the difference is key to accurate break-even analysis:

üè¢ Fixed Costs

Stay the same regardless of how many clients you have.

  • Rent / coworking space
  • Software subscriptions
  • Insurance
  • Your base salary
  • Phone / internet
  • Accounting / legal retainers
  • Marketing (ongoing)

üì¶ Variable Costs

Go up or down based on each client/project.

  • Subcontractor fees
  • Materials / supplies
  • Transaction / payment fees
  • Project-specific software
  • Travel for clients
  • Commission payments
  • Shipping / delivery

üí° Service Business Tip

Many service businesses have very low variable costs ‚Äî sometimes close to $0. If you're a consultant, coach, or fractional executive, your main cost is your time, which is already factored into your fixed costs (your salary). This means your contribution margin is high, and you break even faster.

üíº Pro Tips From the Field

  • Calculate break-even before starting a business. If you need 50 clients/month to break even but can realistically handle 10, your model is broken.
  • Lower fixed costs = lower risk. This is why starting lean matters. Every $500/month you cut moves break-even closer.
  • Raise prices to break even faster. If you charge $1,500 instead of $1,000 per project, you need 33% fewer clients to break even.
  • Retainers accelerate break-even. Three $2,000/month retainers = $6,000 recurring before you even start selling.
  • Track your "break-even date" each month. Celebrate when you hit it ‚Äî everything after is profit.
